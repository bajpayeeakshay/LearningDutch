pronounced differently\[^20\]\[^21\]. Dutch is a **phonetic language** ‚Äî
letters are always pronounced, never silent (unlike English "knight" or
"hour")\[^49\].

you pronounce it. No silent letters!

## 1.2 ‚ö†Ô∏è THE 5 KILLER SOUNDS ‚Äî Special Focus for Indian Speakers

### Sound 1: G / CH ‚Äî The Dutch Throat Sound

-   **What it sounds like:** Like clearing phlegm from your throat /
    gargling
-   **Hindi comparison:** Heavy ‡§ñ (kha) sound from deep in throat
-   **Common mistake:** Indians often say "g" as in "go" ‚Äî this is
    COMPLETELY WRONG
-   **Practice words:** goed (khood), geld (khelt), gaan (khaan), lachen
    (lah-khen), acht (ahkht)
-   **Drill:** Say "Khan" (‡§ñ‡§æ‡§®) ‚Äî that "Kh" sound IS the Dutch
    G\[^54\]\[^20\]

### Sound 2: W ‚Äî The Soft V

-   **What it sounds like:** Place top teeth on lower lip, say "v" very
    softly
-   **Hindi comparison:** ‡§µ (va) ‚Äî soft, not the hard English "W"
-   **Common mistake:** Indians say English "W" (lips rounded) ‚Äî WRONG
-   **Practice words:** werk (verk), water (VAH-ter), wonen (VOH-nen),
    wie (vee), wil (vil)

### Sound 3: UI ‚Äî The Unique Dutch Diphthong

-   **What it sounds like:** Start with "ah" and slide to "ew" while
    pursing lips tight
-   **No equivalent in English or Hindi** ‚Äî must practice!
-   **Practice words:** huis (h≈ìys), tuin (t≈ìyn), fruit (fr≈ìyt), bruid
    (br≈ìyt)
-   **Tip:** Say "ow" (as in "ouch") but with tight, rounded lips\[^14\]

### Sound 4: T and D ‚Äî Dental, Not Retroflex!

-   **Indian speakers' biggest trap:** Hindi has ‡§ü/‡§° (retroflex ‚Äî tongue
    curled back)
-   **Dutch T and D are DENTAL:** Tongue touches the BACK OF YOUR TOP
    TEETH
-   **This is like Hindi ‡§§/‡§¶ (dental stops)**
-   **Practice:** tand (tahnt), doen (doon), datum (DAH-tum), tandarts
    (TAHNT-arts)

### Sound 5: EI / IJ ‚Äî The Long "I" Sound

-   **Pronounced identically** ‚Äî both sound like "i" in English "find"
    or "ay" in "say"
-   **Practice words:** mijn (mine), reis (rayss), ijs (ayss), bij
    (bay), vrij (fray)\[^14\]

## 1.4 Pronunciation Rules That Save You

**Rule 1: Final D sounds like T**

-   goed ‚Üí sounds like "khoot"
-   hond ‚Üí sounds like "hont"
-   BUT: goede ‚Üí "khoo-duh" (D returns when suffix added)\[^25\]

**Rule 2: Final B sounds like P**

-   web ‚Üí sounds like "wep"

**Rule 3: Final V sounds like F, Final Z sounds like S**

-   leven (LAY-ven) but leef (layf)

**Rule 4: The unstressed E always sounds like "uh"**

-   lopen = LOH-puh (not LOH-pen)
-   spreken = SPRAY-kuh

**Rule 5: Drop the final N in speech (advanced tip)**

-   In casual spoken Dutch, "lopen" sounds like "loh-puh" (the final n
    is almost silent)\[^57\]
