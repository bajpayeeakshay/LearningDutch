## 1.4 Pronunciation Rules That Save You

**Rule 1: Final D sounds like T**

-   goed â†’ sounds like "khoot"
-   hond â†’ sounds like "hont"
-   BUT: goede â†’ "khoo-duh" (D returns when suffix added)\[^25\]

**Rule 2: Final B sounds like P**

-   web â†’ sounds like "wep"

**Rule 3: Final V sounds like F, Final Z sounds like S**

-   leven (LAY-ven) but leef (layf)

**Rule 4: The unstressed E always sounds like "uh"**

-   lopen = LOH-puh (not LOH-pen)

**Rule 5: Drop the final N in speech (advanced tip)**

-   In casual spoken Dutch, "lopen" sounds like "loh-puh" (the final n
    is almost silent)\[^57\]
